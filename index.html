Game Design

Project "Wheelin' and Mealin'"

Whealin' and Mealin' is a co-op, online multiplayer, party game where players take on the role of a driver and cook duo aiming to serve delicious food to the denizens of the city.  I work with the design team to ideate on the interplay between cooking and driving.  I also prototype specific features, like car traffic on the city streets, to aid in iteration and evaluate game feel.

Mochi Madness

Mochi Madness is a competitve, 2-player, 3D action game where you play as stick-wielding mochi trying to make the perfect dango stick.  I was a co-designer and the main engineer for this game.  I programmed most of the core mechanics of the game, such as the poking & throwing  of objects, and the unique effects each interactable object has on the players.

Serpent Showdown

I worked with the design team to create a 3D arena fighter, intended to release on the Nintendo Switch. I designed and tuned the combat mechanics, including a "reflect" that knocks projectiles back at the opponent. I also communicated with engineering, art, and usability to refine the game's feel and accessibility. I worked with Unity to greybox the arena, test mechanics, and implement features.

Solo Projects

As a student I have re-created many classic arcade games using C++

Super Mario Bros. 

I implemented a 2D parallax camera and collision boxes for both players and enemies.
I handled all the enemy behaviors as well as sounds, animation, and the win/lose states.

Platform: Visual Studio
Development time: 1 week

The Legend of Zelda

I used a tile map csv file to create a 2D dungeon and spawn enemies.
I implemented collectibles, such as a key, that the player could pick up and use later.
I programmed different enemy behaviors, including the boss who has a normal state and a rage state.

Platform: Visual Studio
Development time: 1 week

Space Tunnel

I implemented a 3D camera that follows the ship as it moves forward through the corridor.
I continuously generate more obstacles and game objects in front of the ship as it continues forward to create an endless mode.
I also recursively programmed chain explosions for certain obstacles.

Platform: Visual Studio
Development time: 1 week
